Enterprise modernization often fails when teams confuse legacy technology with legacy business process.

## Start with workflow gravity

The core question is not _which framework do we prefer_, but _which workflow cannot afford instability_.

That pushes the architecture toward:

- explicit workflow boundaries
- clear transactional rules
- integration adapters that absorb vendor complexity

## Preserve what is trustworthy

Stable integrations and well-understood approval paths should be wrapped, not casually replaced.

Incremental modernization works better when teams:

1. isolate business rules from transport concerns
2. reduce spreadsheet and email dependency first
3. strengthen auditability before expanding scope

## Communication matters

In regulated environments, the modernization plan has to make sense to product owners, operations, and compliance stakeholders.

That is why architecture diagrams alone are not enough. Decision logs and rollout narratives are just as important.
