Not every data problem needs a large platform. Sometimes the right answer is a disciplined utility with excellent failure behavior.

## Make errors legible

Data utilities should fail in ways that help engineers and analysts act quickly.

Useful guardrails include:

- validation summaries that point to the broken contract
- versioned output artifacts
- deterministic CLI execution for CI and scheduled jobs

## Optimize for repeatability

Linux-friendly tooling matters because the utility will eventually live in automation, not only on a developer machine.

That means designing for:

- explicit inputs and outputs
- reproducible execution
- clear logs with enough context to debug the feed

## Keep the solution proportional

Senior engineering judgment shows up in restraint.

The utility should solve the delivery risk cleanly without expanding into a platform no one wanted to own.
